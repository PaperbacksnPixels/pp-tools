The copy problem
every author has.

You can write 80,000 words about the interior life of a fictional person but ask you to write a three-sentence event description and you'd rather clean the bathroom.

You try AI. It gives you something that starts with "I'm thrilled to announce" and ends with "don't miss this opportunity." You edit it for an hour. It still doesn't sound like you. You post it anyway, feeling vaguely embarrassed.

The problem isn't the AI. It's that you never told it who you are or what you actually sound like. These tools fix that. They ask the right questions first — about your voice, your book, your reader, your event — and then generate copy that's calibrated to you specifically.

No press release language. No algorithm-worship. No "unlocking your potential." Just copy that sounds like a smart, specific person wrote it — because in a sense, you did.

Six tools. One suite.

Use them in order, or jump to whatever you need right now.

Tool 01
Find Your Voice
Start here
Seven questions and your copy will never sound generic again.
Answer questions about your tone, pace, how close you get to your reader, and what you never want to sound like. Optionally paste a writing sample. Get back a precise voice profile — one line you can paste into any of the other tools so every output is calibrated to you from the start.
Tool 02
Author Launch Pad
Event copy
You have an event. You need copy. You have thirty minutes.
Works for book launches, readings, workshops, panels, and book clubs. Fill in your event details and get a full Lu.ma event page (title, tagline, description, what-to-expect, author bio), three emails (announce, nudge, last call), and Instagram and LinkedIn captions — all in one shot.
Tool 03
Book Promotion Copy
Ongoing promo
For when you just need people to read the book — no event attached.
Tell it what your book is really about (the emotional premise, not the plot summary), who it's for, and where the book is in its life. Get three emails, Instagram and LinkedIn captions, a one-line pitch, a pitch paragraph for press and podcast outreach, and a podcast guest bio. Ready to use or lightly edit.
Tool 04
Workshop Launcher
Sell a workshop
You have an idea for a workshop. It needs to exist in the world by Friday.
Describe your workshop idea and choose your audience — other authors or readers. Get sales copy, a Payhip product description with bullet list, and three launch emails. The copy doesn't treat your audience like they need convincing — it treats them like they're already the kind of person who would want this.
Tool 05
Find Your Comp Authors
Querying / pitching
Comps you can defend — and a clear list of what to stop using.
Describe your book and the authors you love. Get 4-5 appropriate comp authors with a one-line reason and 2-3 sentences you can use to defend each one in a query letter or agent conversation. Two pitch lines. And a specific avoid list — including blockbusters, books over five years old, and authors at a very different career level, with an explanation of why each one is a mistake.
Tool 06
Substack Strategy Builder
Newsletter
A newsletter strategy built around your actual life, not an ideal one.
Tell it what you write, who your ideal subscriber is, what you want Substack to do for you, and — crucially — how much time you actually have. Get content pillars with specific post ideas, a realistic publishing cadence, a growth strategy specific to where you're starting, honest monetization advice, and a welcome email written in your voice.
